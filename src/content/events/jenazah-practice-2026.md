A funeral rites practice session was held by the Indonesian Muslim Family of Ishikawa (KMII — Keluarga Muslim Indonesia Ishikawa).

The program began with a lecture by Ustadz Ilyas Maulana on the etiquette and procedures for handling the deceased. The practice session took place in two separate areas at Masjid Umar bin Khatab — the sisters practiced on the first floor while the brothers gathered on the second floor.

We learned that caring for the deceased is not as difficult as one might imagine. For example: must the shroud consist of exactly 3 pieces for men and 5 for women? No. Must there be exactly 5 knots? No. Must the person performing the ritual washing be Muslim with a specific intention? Also no.

We cannot cover every detail in this article, but we hope that future sessions will reach even more members of our community and that more brothers and sisters will benefit from these valuable programs.
